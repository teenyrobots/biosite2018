Bio

Joyce Rice is an experience designer, cartoonist, and game designer with a focus in journalism and education. She makes comics, games, and other digital products to help folks tell better stories.

In 2012, she cofounded Symbolia, a comics journalism magazine for iPad, where she was Creative Director for three years. Alongside Editor + Publisher Erin Polgreen, she produced hundreds of pages of comics journalism with contributors from all over the world. Symbolia worked with other media organizations such as Tumblr, Popular Science, the Harvard Business Review, and more to bring interactive comics journalism to new audiences.

In 2015, she was awarded a Journalism Leadership Transformation (JoLT) Fellowship at the American University Game Lab in partnership with the Knight Foundation. JoLT explored the potential for intersection between game design and news design. The JoLT team produced new media solutions for organizations like Vox Media, WAMU, and the Newseum, as well as organizing conferences and workshops for high-level media executives to collaborate and learn new skills.

During the summer, she is a lecturuer in the California College of the Arts where she teaches cartoonists and young designers how to code so they can share their own digital stories.

Her work has been written about by Newsweek, Fast Company, and Citylab. She has spoken about media innovation at SXSW, Game Developers Conference, and on The Verge's Top Shelf. She has worked with clients like PBS, Bed Bath & Beyond, Showtime, and more.
